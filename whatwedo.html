Trading Crude Oil And Refined Petroleum Products

Baltic Alliance focuses trade on crude oil, fuel oil, and naphtha, and we trade the entire barrel. Volumes are sourced through our relationships with producers and refineries, and are supplied directly to the end user. We build and nurture strong relationships with key producers and end users to ensure continuity and fairness of sourcing the resources to operate in the riskiest project environments and realize economies of scale that were not available to the majors.

The company sells crude oil and natural gas from its producing operations under a variety of contractual obligations, some of which may specify the delivery of a fixed and determinable quantity for periods longer than one year. Baltic Alliance also enters into crude oil and petroleum products sales contracts where the source of the products used to fulfill the contract can be a combination of our own production and the spot market

The company traded over 70 million metric tons of crude oil and 65 million metric tons of petroleum product in the year 2020. Third party trading activity represented over 50% of the company's total transactions.

Baltic Alliance continues to market non-Russian crude oil, operates and optimizes its refineries and retail network in Europe and trade non–Russian crude and petroleum products globally in full compliance with all existing and applicable regulatory requirements. Baltic Alliance is sustainably continuing its operations including shipping, in all countries and regions where they are present, committed to the Company’s primary mission of a reliable and compliant supplier of energy to consumers around the world.

We are proud to announce our unwavering commitment to servicing our valued customers with creative and profitable energy solutions. Through our commercial excellence and skills, we are dedicated to delivering innovative and sustainable energy options that meet the evolving needs of our customers and contribute to a greener future.

Logistics management plays a crucial role in the crude oil business, ensuring the efficient and timely movement of crude oil from production sites to refineries or other destinations is our top priority.

TRANSPORTATION

The transportation of Crude oil and petroleum products by rail to designated loading ports is carried out in specialized tank cars. High-quality sheet steel with a thickness of 8 millimeters is used for their manufacturing. The tanks have vapor and oil-benzene resistant internal coatings, as well as high levels of spark and electrostatic safety.

Typically, oil and petroleum products are delivered in tanks with a capacity ranging from 100,000 to 200,000 metric tons. The filling of the products is done from the top, while the unloading is done from the bottom through special openings. According to the regulations, every tank designated for the transportation of oil and petroleum products must have:

A viewing platform

Internal and external staircases

Drainage equipment

Crude oil logistics involve choosing appropriate modes of transportation, such as pipelines, tankers, railcars, or trucks, based on factors like distance, volume, and infrastructure availability. Efficient transportation planning and management help optimize costs, minimize transit times, and ensure the safe delivery of crude oil.

RAILWAY TRANSPORTATION

Bulk transportation: Rail transport is well-suited for bulk transportation of oil and gas. Tank cars designed specifically for these commodities can carry large volumes of liquid or gaseous hydrocarbons in a single shipment. Flexibility in routes: Rail networks offer extensive coverage, allowing for transportation to various regions, including remote areas where pipelines may not be available or economically feasible. This flexibility enables oil and gas to reach destinations that may not be easily accessible through other modes of transport.

Intermodal connectivity: Rail transport often integrates seamlessly with other modes of transport, such as pipelines, ships, or trucks. This intermodal connectivity facilitates the efficient movement of oil and gas between different transportation networks and helps optimize the overall supply chain. Safety measures: Railway companies have specific safety protocols and regulations in place for the transportation of hazardous materials, including oil and gas. Tank cars used for oil and gas shipments are designed and maintained to meet stringent safety standards, reducing the risk of leaks or accidents during transit.

Reliability and scheduling: Rail transport offers reliable scheduling and adherence to timetables, ensuring timely delivery of oil and gas shipments. This predictability is crucial for industries that require a steady and consistent supply of these commodities. Cost-effectiveness: Rail transport can often provide a cost-effective solution for transporting oil and gas, especially for medium to long distances. Compared to other modes of transport, such as trucks or air freight, rail transport can offer competitive rates, particularly for large-scale shipments.

CRUDE OIL EXPORT BY SEA

Most of our crude oil exports are carried out by sea because the maritime transport allows for the transportation of large volumes of crude oil and gas due to the significant capacity of oil tankers and gas carriers. These specialized vessels are designed to handle the specific requirements and volumes of these commodities. Sea transportation provides the company access to a wide range of global destinations. Crude oil and gas can be transported across oceans and reach various ports worldwide, Vvessels can be rerouted to accommodate shifts in supply and demand patterns, ensuring efficient delivery to different destinations and allowing for international trade and distribution.

Safety Measures: The maritime industry follows strict safety regulations and protocols to ensure the safe transportation of crude oil and gas. Tankers are equipped with advanced safety systems and undergo regular inspections to minimize the risk of accidents or spills. The company continues to abide by these safety regulations and protocols due to the hazardous nature of crude oil and gas cargoes, tankers are designed with appropriate safety features and equipment to handle these commodities safely, including proper ventilation, heating, and containment systems.

Storage and Terminal Management: Crude oil often requires storage at various stages of the supply chain. The company's logistics solutions encompasses managing storage facilities, including tank farms and terminals, to ensure proper inventory management, efficient loading and unloading operations, and adherence to safety and environmental regulations. Supply Chain Visibility: We're always utilizing advanced technologies and systems, logistics management solutions to provide real-time visibility into the supply chain. This includes tracking the movement of crude oil shipments, monitoring inventory levels, and providing insights into potential disruptions or bottlenecks. Enhanced visibility helps optimize planning, scheduling, and decision-making processes.

Inventory Management: Effective inventory management is critical in our business operation to balance supply and demand. The company's Logistics solutions employ inventory tracking systems and forecasting tools to monitor crude oil levels, optimize storage capacities, and ensure timely replenishment. This helps prevent stockouts, minimize storage costs, and maintain operational efficiency.

Documentation and Compliance: The company's logistics management solutions facilitate the handling of documentation and compliance requirements related to crude oil transportation. This includes managing bills of lading, customs documentation, regulatory permits, and compliance with safety and environmental regulations. Automated systems can streamline documentation processes, reducing errors and delays.

EXPLORATION AND PRODUCTION

Baltic Alliance has been carrying out activities in the Exploration & Production sector, The Company engages in oil and gas production. It owns a 5% minority stake in TengizChevroil, which produces oil from the Tengiz field, and a 12.5% interest in the Caspian Pipeline Consortium (CPC). CPC is the route for more than two thirds of Kazakhstan's total oil exports. It shipped 60.7 million tons in 2021. It is more than 1,500 km (930 miles) long and exports oil from Western Kazakhstan and Russian oil producers via the Black Sea. It is regarded as the largest international oil transportation project

Its main shareholders are Russian oil pipeline monopoly Transnet with 24%, Kazakhstan's KazMunayGas (19%), Chevron Caspian Pipeline Consortium Company (15%) BALTIC ALLIANCE B.V (12.5%), Mobil Caspian Pipeline Company (7.5%) CPC Company (7%) and Resent-Shell Caspian Ventures Limited (7.5%).

We are also a co-operator of the Karachaganak producing field and a part of the North Caspian Sea PSA (NCSPSA) consortium. In 2018 we became joint operator in the exploration block Isatay with KMG . In July 2019, KMG, Ministry of Energy of the Republic of Kazakhstan and Baltic Alliance signed a contract for the exploration and production of hydrocarbons at Abay Block, which makes IOC the first joint venture holding two licenses in Kazakhstan. In 2021, the development activities of the Kashagan field (Baltic Alliance's interest 16.81%) concerned the phased expansion program of production capacity. The ongoing activities, sanctioned in 2020, mainly concerned increasing gas reinjection capacity by means of upgrading the existing facilities and delivering a part of gas volumes to a new onshore treatment unit operated by a third party within the gas treatment expansion projects of the Karachaganak field.

Kazakhstan holds the ninth place in the world according to the proven oil reserves. It is established that there are 6.5 billion tons of oil reserves in the depths of Kazakhstan subsoil and these stocks are projected to reach a mark of 20-25 billion tons. Steady growth of the country and citizens’ welfare, great prospects, developing economy, international cooperation and, of course, the recognition of Kazakhstan by International community- all this is achieved through the company's contribution to the development of Kazakhstan’s oil industry.

In Kazakhstan, the first is the initial development of the giant offshore Kashagan field which is included in the North Caspian Production Sharing Agreement in which Baltic Alliance participates. The second is the Tengizchevroil joint venture which includes a production license in the Tengiz field and the nearby Korolev field. The joint venture is producing and proved undeveloped reserves will continue to move to proved developed as approved development phases progress. The third is the Groningen gas field in the Netherlands. Proved undeveloped reserves reported for this field are related to installation of future stages of compression. These reserves will move to proved developed when the additional stages of compression are installed to maintain field delivery pressure.
